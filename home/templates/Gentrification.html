Gentrification

{% include 'nav.html' %}

Gentrification is the process of renovating and improving a house or district so that it conforms to middle-class taste. Gentrification is booming all around the U.S Places all around DC look completely different from how it looked 10 years ago even from 3 years ago. The majority of residents or former residents can not recognize certain areas because of the gentrified changes.So many of the run down buildings or houses in the district started to transform into new buildings including very expensive condos/apartments.

Why this is important

Not only do people need to take note on what's changing in their community they need to evaluate if the changes are beneficial to them and others in their community. Many look at gentrification in a, negative way, some believe it is taking away the culture. There is a debate on if gentrification plays a part in racial or economical issues. There is no absolute answer since answers are based on an opinion and how that person benefits from the changes in their community.It is important to interview people so the users can see both, the negative and positive sides of what gentrification brings. For example a person who doesn't come from money whose rent is now high can be a negatively impacted, while a family who kids can now walk down a certain street without thinking they are automatically in danger is a positive aspect. Two different stories with two different outcomes, like the saying "there is two sides to every story‚Äù and this website is here to reveal both.

Video Description

This video highlights the different opinions and insight of how gentrification is either beneficial or detrimental. The different interviewers all come from different backgrounds and areas in the district. Some benefit from the changes that happened in their community while others not so much. The goal was to give every participant the chance to explain and identify their feelings on the topic. Many have expressed that they believe gentrification is both a negative as well as positive attribute to their community. Everyone who participated understood that even in situations they benefited from others had to pay a cost. The video not only shines a light on the beliefs and opinions of DC residents, it gives a sense of understanding that not just one race or a specific minority can be affected by gentrification because at the end of the day DC is the place we all call home.
