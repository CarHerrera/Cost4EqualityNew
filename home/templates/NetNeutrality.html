Net Neutrality

{% include 'nav.html'%}

Net Neutrality Is the principle that the all data is treated fairly and that Internet service providers(ISP) cannot create fast lanes or slow lanes, throttle internet traffic, block access from certain websites, and they cannot charge extra for using certain websites or to certain users. Net Neutrality keeps the internet fair and equal for all internet users, and who doesn't enjoy fairness and equality on something as free and useful as the Internet? Without net neutrality regulating ISP’s, they have the ability to charge you extra for just accessing certain websites, or slow down access to certain websites, or any website that otherwise would’ve been completely open to anybody who could have come across the website. Not only can they charge you extra just for accessing certain websites, they can control what you see on the internet, making the internet less like a place with freedom of choice, and more of a place with an illusion of choice. With technology rapidly advancing and the internet becoming more and more of an essential tool, it makes no sense to try and place roadblocks to restrict the rapidly growing creativity and innovation that the internet has had and been known for ever since the Internet had been widely popularized. If the repeal of net neutrality remains, and companies begin to charge extra just to access the internet and certain websites, how much will you be willing to pay?

Future without Net Neutrality

For as long as the Internet has existed it has flourished with its constant streams of creativity, its freedom of navigation from one website to the next, and its eventual innate ability to share whatever you wanted, to whoever wanted to see it. Net neutrality has been the secret hand that has guided the internet through its age of flourishment, and without it the Internet will face constriction that ISPs will Inevitably force upon the Internet. Without Net neutrality ISPs have the ability to charge you extra, the ability to block websites from users, and they can throttle your internet, meaning that they can control the speed that your computer accesses certain websites. With the ability to block content, and force content, ISPs will make the internet less of the internet, and make the internet seem like it’s cable tv. The Internet would no longer be a place of freedom of navigation, and a place where each site loads equally and fairly to one another, but a place with an illusion of navigation and slow lanes and fast lanes to those who are willing to pay for those fast lanes.
